About Me

Professional Background & Academic Journey

Professional Summary

Ph.D.-qualified academician with over 25 years of comprehensive teaching and academic experience in Computer Science and Engineering.

My career has been dedicated to advancing the field of Computer Science through rigorous research, innovative teaching methodologies, and active participation in the academic community. With expertise spanning Distributed Systems, Delay Tolerant Networks (DTNs), Secure Data Sharing Architectures, Network Latency Analysis, and Cryptographic Access Control, I have contributed significantly to both theoretical and applied aspects of computer science.

Throughout my career, I have maintained a strong commitment to undergraduate teaching, curriculum delivery, and student mentoring. My research contributions have been presented at numerous national and international conferences, and published in reputed journals. I actively participate in faculty development programs and academic leadership activities aligned with AICTE, UGC, and university frameworks.

Research Interests

Distributed Systems

Focus on Delay Tolerant Networks (DTNs) and their applications in challenged network environments

Network Security

Cryptographic Access Control mechanisms, particularly CP-ABE (Ciphertext-Policy Attribute-Based Encryption)

Data Sharing Architectures

Secure data sharing solutions in constrained and intermittent connectivity scenarios

Network Performance

Latency analysis, routing optimization, and throw-box deployment strategies in DTNs

Educational Technology

ICT tools integration in academic environments and systems-level computing education

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching approach emphasizes bridging theoretical foundations with practical applications. I believe in fostering critical thinking, encouraging research-oriented learning, and preparing students not just for their first job, but for a lifetime career in technology.

Through mentoring, curriculum innovation, and active engagement with students, I strive to create an environment where academic excellence meets real-world problem-solving.
